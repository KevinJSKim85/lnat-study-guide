How to use this site

  1. Start here — read the format and scoring above, then take the quick diagnostic below.
  2. Learn — work through the 14 Section A lessons, grouped by skill cluster.
  3. Practice — drill any question type with instant self-marking and worked solutions.
  4. Mocks — sit a full timed paper, then review every question.
  5. Essays — study the fundamentals, theme argument banks, model essays, and write to the clock.
  6. Schedule — follow the 10-week intensive plan day by day.
  7. Reference — search the glossary, recommended reading, and FAQ.
  8. Progress — track accuracy by type and mock scores; export a backup any time.

Learn — Section A lessons

All 14 lessons, grouped by skill cluster. Each lesson covers what the type is, how to recognise it, a worked example, the common traps, and a strategy. Select a lesson to open it.

Practice — drills

Drill questions by type and difficulty. Pick an option and submit: the engine marks instantly and reveals why each option is right or wrong, plus a worked solution. The correct answer is not present on the page until you submit. Mock items stay unseen — these are dedicated practice questions.

Mocks

Seven full timed papers. Each is Section A (42 questions, 95 minutes, no negative marking) followed by Section B (one essay from three prompts, 40 minutes). After Section A you get a score out of 42 with a band label and a per-type breakdown, and you can review every question with its rationale.

Mock

Essays — Section B

Section B is self-assessed against a rubric; it is not auto-graded. The mechanical checklist below catches structural and surface problems reliably, but whether your argument persuades a reader who does not share your assumptions needs a real human reader.

Six theme argument banks: key positions, counter-positions, concrete examples, and traceable facts you can deploy in an essay.

The full prompt bank. Official LNAT prompts are clearly labelled and cited; all others are original / illustrative.

12 model essays and 6 weak essays. Each is shown with its annotations — every annotation names a rubric dimension or a Five-I pitfall and explains how the essay hits or fails it.

Pick a prompt, start the 40-minute Section B timer, write in the box, and on finish the self-marking checklist appears. Your essay text stays in your browser only.

Schedule — 10-week intensive plan

A day-by-day plan to an early-September 2026 test date. If targeting Oxford or Cambridge (sit by 15 October 2026), shift every week one week later.

Reference

Flip through the glossary as flashcards. Click the card (or press Space) to reveal the definition, then mark Got it or Review again. Cards you mark for review come back at the end. Use the arrow keys or Enter to move on.
